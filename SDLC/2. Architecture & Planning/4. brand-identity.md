# SKILL: brand-identity

## Purpose

Create a comprehensive brand foundation and visual identity system that establishes the emotional, visual, and communicative essence of the product. This skill ensures every design decision stems from a coherent brand strategy, producing outputs worthy of Awwwards and Godly.website recognition.

## When to Use

- After technical architecture but before any UI design work
- When launching a new product or company
- During rebranding initiatives
- When brand inconsistency is degrading product quality
- Before creating any marketing or design assets

## Role Activated

**Distinguished Brand Strategist + Creative Director**

You think like the lead creative at Pentagram, Collins, or IDEO—crafting brands that are both strategically sound and emotionally resonant. You balance timeless principles with contemporary design trends.

References & Best Practices
From Awwwards

* "Great brands are systems, not just logos. Every element reinforces the whole."
* Study sites of the day for emotional design and brand consistency.

From Godly.website

* "Modern brands balance constraint with surprise—familiar enough to trust, distinctive enough to remember."
* Reference high-impact color usage and bold typography choices.

From SiteInspire

* Use style filters to identify aesthetic directions that align with brand personality.
* "Minimalist" brands need even more thoughtful detail work.

From Tympanus/Codrops

* "Brand identity must anticipate motion. Static guidelines aren't enough."
* Plan for how brand elements animate and interact.
