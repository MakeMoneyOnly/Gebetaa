## Product Strategy Foundation

### Company/Product Mission

**"Empower restaurants in emerging markets to thrive in the digital age with affordable, intelligent infrastructure that increases revenue, reduces costs, and builds lasting customer relationships."**

We exist to democratize access to restaurant technology that was previously only available to large chains with deep pockets. By building a lightweight, intelligent platform specifically designed for emerging market realities, we help small and medium restaurants compete, grow, and succeed.

### Problem Statement

Restaurants in emerging markets face significant operational challenges:

1. **Inflation-Driven Price Changes:** Ethiopia's food inflation was 32.3% (Jan 2024) and still 15.7% (Jan 2025). Restaurants update prices 2-5 times monthly, costing $200-800 per batch in printing costs.

2. **Poor Upselling:** Manual upselling is inconsistent. Best servers upsell 40-60% of tables; average servers only 10-20%. Restaurants lose 10-15% potential revenue.

3. **Lack of Dish-Level Insights:** 64% of restaurants prioritize analytics but can't afford expensive platforms ($200-500/month). They operate with gut feeling rather than data.

4. **Multi-Location Management Chaos:** Chains struggle with brand consistency, location-specific pricing, and centralized management across 5-10+ locations.

5. **Loss of Customer Data to Aggregators:** Aggregators charge 20-30% commission and provide zero customer data. Restaurants can't build CRM or run targeted promotions.

**Current Solutions Fail Because:**
- Enterprise POS (Toast, Square): Too expensive ($200-500/month), over-engineered, no emerging market focus
- Basic digital menus (Menu Tiger, MyDigiMenu): No intelligence, poor analytics, limited features

---

### Solution Overview

**Gebeta** is a cloud-based, multi-tenant SaaS platform that provides:

1. **QR-based Digital Menus** with real-time price updates
2. **Smart Upselling Engine** using frequency-based and category-based recommendations
3. **Menu Intelligence Dashboard** showing performance quadrants
4. **Multi-Location Management** with location-level controls
5. **Offline-First Architecture** for unreliable internet
6. **Local Payment Integration** (Telebirr, M-Pesa, etc.)
7. **Multi-Language Support** (Amharic, Swahili, Arabic, English)

**Technical Stack:**
- Frontend: React + TypeScript
- Backend: Supabase (PostgreSQL + Realtime)
- Hosting: Vercel (frontend) + Supabase (backend)
- Payments: Local payment providers (Telebirr, M-Pesa)
- Analytics: Custom dashboard with Supabase queries

### User Stories

#### Epic 1: Digital Menu Management

**As a restaurant owner, I want to create and manage a digital menu so that customers can view items and prices in real-time.**

**User Stories:**

1. **US-1.1:** As a restaurant owner, I want to add menu items with name, description, price, and image so that my menu is complete.
   - **Acceptance Criteria:**
     - Can add item with required fields (name, description, price, category)
     - Can upload image (optional)
     - Can set availability (in stock / sold out)
     - Item appears in digital menu immediately
     - Validation prevents incomplete items

2. **US-1.2:** As a restaurant owner, I want to organize items into categories so that customers can navigate the menu easily.
   - **Acceptance Criteria:**
     - Can create categories (Appetizers, Mains, Drinks, Desserts)
     - Can assign items to categories
     - Can reorder categories
     - Categories display in digital menu

3. **US-1.3:** As a restaurant owner, I want to update prices in real-time so that customers always see accurate pricing.
   - **Acceptance Criteria:**
     - Can update item price
     - Price changes reflect immediately in digital menu
     - Price history is tracked
     - Bulk price update capability

4. **US-1.4:** As a restaurant owner, I want to generate QR codes for tables so that customers can scan and view the menu.
   - **Acceptance Criteria:**
     - Can generate QR code for restaurant
     - Can generate QR codes for individual tables
     - QR codes link to restaurant-specific menu
     - QR codes can be downloaded as PNG

5. **US-1.5:** As a customer, I want to scan a QR code and view the menu on my phone so that I can see items and prices.
   - **Acceptance Criteria:**
     - QR code opens mobile-friendly menu
     - Menu loads quickly on 3G connections
     - Menu displays items, descriptions, prices, images
     - Menu works on older Android phones (6.0+)
     - Menu works offline (cached)

**As a restaurant owner, I want to provide smart upselling suggestions so that customers add more items to their orders.**

**User Stories:**

1. **US-2.1:** As a restaurant owner, I want to see upselling suggestions based on frequently ordered together items so that I can increase AOV.

     - Can set priority of suggestions

3. **US-2.3:** As a customer, I want to see relevant upselling suggestions so that I can discover items I might like.
   - **Acceptance Criteria:**
     - Suggestions appear when viewing items
     - Maximum 2 suggestions per item
     - Suggestions are easy to dismiss
     - Suggestions are relevant to viewed item

4. **US-2.4:** As a restaurant owner, I want to track upselling conversion rates so that I know what's working.
   - **Acceptance Criteria:**

     - Data updates in real-time

### Assumptions and Risks

#### Key Assumptions

1. **Pricing Assumption:** Restaurants will pay $10-50/month for digital menu + analytics
   - **Validation:** User interviews with 8-12 restaurant owners
   - **Confidence:** Medium (7/10)
   - **Mitigation:** Free trial to test value, flexible pricing tiers

2. **Upselling Assumption:** Smart upselling increases AOV by 19-30%
   - **Validation:** Research shows 10-15% overall increase
   - **Confidence:** High (8/10)
   - **Mitigation:** A/B testing in MVP, track actual results

3. **Multi-Location Assumption:** Multi-location management is a priority for chains
   - **Validation:** Chain manager persona critical need
   - **Confidence:** High (8/10)
   - **Mitigation:** User interviews with chain managers

4. **Offline Assumption:** Offline-first architecture is critical for target markets
   - **Validation:** Internet reliability issues in markets
   - **Confidence:** High (9/10)
   - **Mitigation:** Technical validation, user testing

5. **Payment Assumption:** Local payment integration is necessary for adoption
   - **Validation:** M-Pesa, Telebirr dominant in markets
   - **Confidence:** High (9/10)
   - **Mitigation:** Partner with local payment providers

---

#### Key Risks

1. **Adoption Risk:** 20% of diners actively dislike QR menus
   - **Impact:** High
   - **Probability:** Medium
   - **Mitigation:** Support physical + digital menus, focus on convenience not replacement

2. **Technical Risk:** Offline-first architecture adds complexity
   - **Impact:** Medium
   - **Probability:** Medium
   - **Mitigation:** Use proven patterns (Service Workers, IndexedDB), extensive testing

3. **Market Risk:** Ethiopia's inflation declining (reducing urgency)
   - **Impact:** Medium
   - **Probability:** Low
   - **Mitigation:** Position as "omnichannel price consistency" not just inflation management

4. **Competition Risk:** Local players may emerge
   - **Impact:** Medium
   - **Probability:** Medium
   - **Mitigation:** First-mover advantage, build data moat, focus on intelligence

5. **Payment Risk:** Local payment integration complexity varies by country
   - **Impact:** High
   - **Probability:** Medium
   - **Mitigation:** Partner with established providers, start with one country
