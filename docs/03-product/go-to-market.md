# ገበጣ lole — Go-To-Market Strategy

**Version 1.0 · March 2026 · Confidential**

> This document covers how lole gets from 0 to 200 restaurants. It assumes the founder is the sales team. No sales hires until Month 9 at the earliest. Every strategy in this document is executable by one person with a phone, Telegram, and a working product.

---

## The Fundamental GTM Insight

Ethiopian restaurants do not buy software from websites. They buy from people they know, or from people referred by people they know. Cold outreach via email or LinkedIn does not work here. The entire GTM strategy is built around this reality.

**The unit of trust in Addis is a relationship, not a product feature.**

Your first 5 restaurants will come from your personal network. Your next 45 will come from those 5 telling their friends. Your next 150 will come from visible success — "I saw that restaurant using a tablet, I want that."

## Target Customer Segmentation

### Segment 1 — Sweet Spot (Target: 70% of first 200 restaurants)

**Who:** Independent sit-down restaurants and cafés in Addis, 30–150 covers, 1 location, ETB 30,000–200,000/month revenue.

**Key characteristics:**

- Owner is on-site or checks in daily
- Currently uses paper or a basic cash register
- Has at least one staff member comfortable with a smartphone
- Accepts or wants to accept Telebirr
- Likely in Bole, Kazanchis, CMC, Gerji, Megenagna, Sarbet, Arat Kilo, or Piazza

**Willingness to pay:** ETB 800–1,500/month. Pro plan (1,200 ETB) is within range.

**Acquisition channel:** Direct founder outreach → peer referral

---

### Segment 2 — Enterprise Anchor (Target: 10–15% of first 200 restaurants)

**Who:** Hotel restaurants, café chains (3–10 locations), corporate canteens, airport food outlets.

**Key characteristics:**

- Finance department requires ERCA compliance
- IT department wants API integration (accounting software, ERP)
- Multiple locations need consolidated reporting
- Staff turnover is high → Amharic UI reduces training time significantly
- Decision maker is a GM or Finance Director, not the owner

**Willingness to pay:** ETB 3,500–10,000/month per location (Business / Enterprise plan).

**Acquisition channel:** Direct founder pitch to GM/Finance. Referral from an existing enterprise client.

**These accounts have 5–10× the revenue of a sweet-spot account and anchor your MRR from month 1.**

---

### Segment 3 — Long Tail (Target: 15–20% of first 200 restaurants)

**Who:** Fast-casual, quick-service, and kiosk operations. Street-level cafés and juice bars.

**Key characteristics:**

- High order volume, low ticket size
- Staff has minimal tech literacy
- Often cash-only today but interested in Telebirr
- May not need all 14 dashboard features

**Willingness to pay:** Free plan or ETB 600–800/month (below Pro rate).

**Strategy:** Serve this segment with the Free plan. They become paying customers if they grow. They provide volume and word-of-mouth in neighbourhoods.

## Phases of Acquisition

### Phase 0 — Founder Network (Month 0–1) · Target: 5 restaurants

**The goal:** Get 5 restaurants live before any marketing. These are your case studies, your testimonials, your proof that the product works in a real Addis kitchen.

**How to find them:**

1. Make a list of every restaurant you personally visit and every restaurant owner you know
2. Ask every contact: "Which restaurant owner do you know who complains about managing orders?"
3. Target restaurants where you can personally walk in and demonstrate the POS in 10 minutes

**The ask in the first meeting:**

> "ይህ ስርዓት ሙሉ ለሙሉ ነፃ ነው — ለሶስት ወራት። ልክ ሳምንቱን ሙሉ አብሮ አብሮ እናሰራ።"
> "This system is completely free — for three months. Let us work together for one week and see."

**What you give the pilot restaurants:**

- Free Pro plan for 3 months
- Founder personally sets up the tablet and Termux print server
- Founder trains the waitstaff (in Amharic) for 2 hours
- Direct Telegram line for support
- Weekly check-in for the first month

**What you need from pilot restaurants:**

- Permission to video their staff using the product (for marketing)
- An honest conversation after 30 days about what is working and what is not
- A referral to one other restaurant owner if they are happy at 60 days

---

### Phase 1 — Referral Engine (Month 1–3) · Target: 5 → 25 restaurants

**The activation moment:** When a pilot restaurant owner tells another owner "you should use lole" without you asking. This is the only metric that matters in Phase 1.

**The referral programme (Horizon 1):**

- Restaurant A refers Restaurant B
- Both get 2 months Pro free when Restaurant B goes live
- No cash, no complex voucher system — just free months
- Tracked manually at this stage (you know every restaurant personally)

**The proof point conversation:**

Every new restaurant conversation starts with:

> "ካፌ ሉቺያ ካለፉት ሁለት ወራት ጀምሮ ይህን ጥቅም ላይ እያዋሉ ነው። ባለቤቱ አቶ X ስልካቸውን ይጠቀሙ።"
> "[Café Lucía] has been using this for two months. The owner [Name] is on their phone — call them."

A warm referral in Addis closes faster than any demo. The product demo is secondary to the trust transfer.

**Neighbourhood clustering strategy:**

Do not spread across all of Addis randomly. Cluster by neighbourhood:

1. Win 3–4 restaurants on one street (e.g., Bole Road)
2. Every passing restaurant owner sees the tablets, the QR menus, the Amharic KDS
3. When you pitch the next restaurant on that street: "Your three neighbours already use this"

Physical visibility is marketing. A guest scanning a lole QR code at a table is an impression.

### Phase 2 — Inbound + Community (Month 3–6) · Target: 25 → 75 restaurants

Once 25 restaurants are live, there is enough social proof to invest in inbound channels.

**Channel 1 — Telegram Channel**

Create "lole Restaurant Tips" (ምግብ ቤት ጠቃሚ ምክሮች) — a Telegram channel with daily/weekly content for Ethiopian restaurant owners:

- "አዲሱ የፀደይ ምናሌ ሀሳቦች" (New spring menu ideas)
- Revenue optimisation tips ("Your busiest hour is probably 12–1PM. Do you have enough staff?")
- ERCA compliance updates
- ETB/USD exchange rate impact on food costs
- lole customer stories (with owner's permission and Amharic quote)

Target: 2,000 subscribers within 6 months. Cost: 0 ETB. Every subscriber is a potential customer.

**Channel 2 — TikTok / Instagram (Amharic)**

Short videos showing:

- A waiter using the Amharic POS ("ትዕዛዝ ለኩሽና ይላካል!" — Order sent to kitchen!)
- Guest scanning a QR code and ordering
- Owner checking revenue on their phone at midnight
- Bilingual receipt printing

Target audience: Restaurant owners, hospitality students, the general Addis audience who sees modern restaurant tech and shares it.

**Channel 3 — Ethiopian Restaurant Association (ምግብ ቤቶች ማህበር)**

Present at one industry association meeting with a live demo. Ethiopian business associations are relationship-intensive — one presentation to 50 restaurant owners is worth 200 cold calls.

**Channel 4 — Hotel procurement networks**

Warm introduction to 2–3 hotel GMs via existing Enterprise clients. Present ERCA compliance, multi-location, and Arabic/English receipt as primary value. Enterprise deal size: 5–10× a single restaurant.

### Phase 3 — Sales System (Month 6–12) · Target: 75 → 200 restaurants

At 75 restaurants, the referral engine is running but a structured sales process is needed to hit 200.

**First sales hire (Month 9):**

- Ethiopian, Addis-based, speaks Amharic fluently
- Restaurant or hospitality background preferred — they understand the job of a waiter and a chef
- OTE structure: ETB 8,000 base + ETB 500 per activated restaurant + ETB 200/month recurring for active restaurants in their book
- Equipped with: a demo tablet (fully set up lole), printed one-pager in Amharic, Telegram access to founder for escalations

**Sales process (standardised):**

```
Day 0:   Warm introduction or walk-in. 5-min context conversation.
         "May I show you something on this tablet for 10 minutes?"

Day 0:   Live demo. Order from Amharic menu. Send to KDS. Show receipt.
         Show analytics on phone. Let them call a reference customer.

Day 1–3: Trial start. Founder or sales rep sets up hardware.
         Trains staff (Amharic, 2 hours). First live orders.

Day 7:   Check-in call. Resolve any issues. Confirm they are using it daily.

Day 30:  Conversion call. "You have processed X orders, ETB Y in revenue.
         Pro plan is 1,200 ETB/month. Shall we activate your subscription?"

Day 60:  Referral ask. "Which other restaurant owner should I call?"
```

**The demo tablet:**

Every sales conversation happens on a real lole tablet — pre-loaded with a demo restaurant menu (in Amharic), with a thermal printer connected. The prospect takes their own order. This is more powerful than any slide deck.

## Pricing & Conversion Strategy

### The Free Plan as a Sales Tool

The Free plan (10 tables, 2 staff, 1 KDS) is not charity — it is a sales channel.

A restaurant on the Free plan will hit the limit the first week they try to add their third waiter or eleventh table. That friction is the conversion moment. The upgrade from Free to Pro happens inside the product — "Upgrade to Pro — 1,200 ETB/month" — and can be completed in 3 minutes via Chapa.

**Free → Pro conversion target: 40% within 60 days of going live.**

### Annual vs Monthly

Offer an annual plan at 10,000 ETB (equivalent to ~2 months free vs. monthly rate of 1,200 ETB × 12 = 14,400 ETB). Annual plans:

- Reduce churn (committed for 12 months)
- Provide upfront cash for operations
- Signal serious intent from the restaurant

**Target: 30% of paying restaurants on annual plans within 6 months.**

### Enterprise Pricing

Enterprise plans are not listed publicly. Every enterprise deal is a negotiation based on:

- Number of locations
- Monthly transaction volume
- Whether ERCA integration is required
- Whether API access is needed for accounting integrations
- SLA requirements

Minimum enterprise deal: 5,000 ETB/month. Average target: 8,000–15,000 ETB/month.

## Customer Success & Retention

### The 3-30-90 Onboarding Framework

| Day        | Action                            | Goal                                                                                                                                 |
| ---------- | --------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Day 3**  | Founder / sales rep check-in call | Confirm: POS used daily? Printer working? Staff comfortable? Resolve blockers.                                                       |
| **Day 30** | Analytics review with owner       | Show them their top items, peak hours, revenue trend. Make the data meaningful. "Your Tibs outsells everything else 3:1 on Fridays." |
| **Day 90** | Expansion conversation            | "You've been on Free for 3 months. Here's what Pro would give you: [analytics, loyalty, inventory alerts]. Ready to upgrade?"        |

### Churn Prevention

Churn signals to watch in the dashboard (admin view, Phase 2):

- Restaurant has not processed an order in 48h during business hours → outreach immediately
- Restaurant processed <20% of their historical order volume this week → outreach
- Restaurant has submitted a support ticket that has been open >24h → escalate to founder

**The hard truth about Ethiopian restaurant churn:** Restaurants close. The industry has high mortality (estimated 30–40% of new restaurants close within 2 years in Addis). lole's churn will always have a floor driven by restaurant closures, not by product dissatisfaction. Track "closed restaurant churn" separately from "voluntary product churn."

## Unit Economics

| Metric                               | Target (Month 6)                                    | Target (Month 12)                    |
| ------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| CAC (cost to acquire one restaurant) | ETB 800 (founder time + demo hardware amortisation) | ETB 1,200 (including sales rep cost) |
| Average MRR per restaurant           | ETB 900 (mix of Free and Pro)                       | ETB 1,100 (higher Pro penetration)   |
| LTV (assuming 18-month avg tenure)   | ETB 16,200                                          | ETB 19,800                           |
| LTV:CAC ratio                        | 20:1                                                | 16:1                                 |
| Payback period                       | <1 month                                            | ~1 month                             |
| Gross margin (SaaS revenue only)     | ~82%                                                | ~80%                                 |

**These are strong SaaS unit economics.** The LTV:CAC ratio of 20:1 is well above the 3:1 minimum benchmark. The challenge is not unit economics — it is velocity. Getting to 200 restaurants requires consistent weekly acquisition, not waiting for inbound.
