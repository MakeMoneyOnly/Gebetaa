## 01 — Brand Strategy

## Mission

To give every restaurant in Ethiopia — from a neighborhood coffee house to a multi-location dining group — the
same world-class operating intelligence that the best restaurants on earth enjoy.

## Vision

A future where no restaurant in Ethiopia loses a sale, a customer, or an hour of productivity to broken, manual,
paper-based systems.

## Brand Positioning

"LoLe is the silent operator behind every successful restaurant."
LoLe occupies the infrastructure layer of the restaurant industry — not a point-of-sale tool, not a loyalty app, but
the complete operating system that unifies every function of a restaurant into one intelligent, reliable platform.
Positioning statement:

One platform. Every operation.

the business.

## Brand Story

"LoLe" comes from Amharic — it means servant or attendant. But LoLe is not a passive servant. LoLe is the
intelligent, tireless system that attends to every detail of a restaurant's operation so the owner, the chef, and the
team can focus on what they do best: creating exceptional experiences for guests.
When a customer scans a QR code to order, LoLe processes it. When the kitchen needs to know what's next, LoLe
displays it. When a waiter deserves a tip, LoLe captures it. When an owner wants to know why Friday sales were
30% above average, LoLe explains it. The restaurant runs. LoLe makes it possible.
This is the transformation LoLe delivers: from manual service to intelligent operations.

## Typographic Principles

## Hierarchy

Always establish clear visual hierarchy: one dominant heading, one
supporting subhead, one body level per section.

## Alignment

Left-align all body text. Center-align only for short hero statements (3
words or fewer per line).

## Contrast

Use weight contrast (bold vs. regular) and size contrast instead of color
contrast for legibility.

## Spacing

Generous line-height (1.6–1.75 for body). Tight tracking for headings (–1
to –2%). Wide tracking for labels (+2 to +4%).

## Design Principles

## 01 · Clarity Over Decoration

Every element must earn its place. Remove anything that does not help the user understand, decide, or act.
Resist the urge to fill space with decorative patterns or gratuitous imagery.

## 02 · Structure Creates Trust

Restaurants trust LoLe with their livelihoods. The design must feel structured, reliable, and precise — like
infrastructure, not a consumer app. Use consistent grid systems, predictable spacing, and rigorous alignment.

## 03 · Calm Intelligence

Complexity should feel effortless. A restaurant owner looking at their dashboard should feel in command, not
overwhelmed. Use progressive disclosure to reveal depth without front-loading complexity.

## 04 · Warm, Not Cold

Infrastructure does not have to feel clinical. LoLe's warm mist backgrounds, rounded components, and human-
first copy give the platform approachability that matches the hospitality industry it serves.

## 05 — Brand Voice & Messaging

Tone of Voice
LoLe speaks like a trusted expert who has seen the problem from both sides — someone who understands the
chaos of a busy restaurant and knows exactly what it takes to solve it. The voice is confident without being
arrogant, clear without being cold, and helpful without being cheerful.
Confident We know this works. We've built it for you. We state facts, not claims.

## Clear

No jargon. No buzzwords. One idea per sentence. The smartest
explanation is the simplest one.
Reliable We say what we'll do, and we do what we say. Every word earns trust.

## Respectful

We treat restaurant owners as intelligent, capable people who deserve
great tools.
Direct We do not hedge, apologize, or over-explain. We get to the point.

## Messaging Principles

Lead with outcomes
Don't say "QR ordering module" — say "Customers order themselves.
Your team serves more."
Specificity wins
Use real numbers when possible. "Less waiting" < "Orders in 45
seconds."
Avoid tech speak
Never use acronyms or technical terms in customer-facing copy without
immediate plain-language context.
Active voice always "LoLe syncs your menu" not "your menu is synced by LoLe."

## 07 — Motion & Interaction

## Animation Principles

## Purpose

Every animation communicates state change or guides attention. Never
decorative.
Tone Smooth and precise. Like a well-tuned machine, not a toy.

## Duration

UI micro-interactions: 100–200ms · Page transitions: 200–350ms ·

## Onboarding: 400–600ms

## Easing

ease-out for enter animations · ease-in for exit · ease-in-out for state
changes

## Restraint

When in doubt, animate less. Busy screens (KDS, POS) must have zero
decorative animation.

## Transitions

Page transitions Fade + subtle vertical slide (8px) — consistent across all routes
Modal open Scale from 0.96 → 1.0 + fade in · 200ms ease-out
Drawer / sidebar Slide in from left or right · 250ms ease-out

## 08 — Photography & Illustration

## Photography Direction

LoLe's photography must reflect real Ethiopian restaurant life — the warmth, the energy, the craftsmanship, and
the pride of the industry. Imagery should feel documentary, not staged.

## Style Parameters

Color grading
Warm, natural color temperature. Slight golden-hour warmth. Avoid
cold, desaturated, or over-filtered looks.

## Lighting

Natural or ambient light preferred. Soft, even exposure. Avoid harsh flash
or clinical lighting.

## Subjects

Restaurant owners, chefs, waiters, customers — all Ethiopian — in
authentic working environments.

## Composition

Rule of thirds. Space for copy overlay when needed. Clean negative space
on mist/warm backgrounds.
Depth of field Moderate bokeh to separate subject from background. Not extreme blur.
Aspect ratios
16:9 for hero banners · 3:2 for editorial · 1:1 for social · 4:5 for Instagram
feed

## Photography Do's

- Authentic moments: staff at work, customers genuinely enjoying meals
- Beautifully composed food shots for contextual use
- Technology in use: tablets on tables, QR codes being scanned, KDS screens in kitchen
- Real restaurant spaces in Addis Ababa — local architecture and interior design
- Diverse representation of Ethiopian hospitality workers and diners

## Photography Don'ts

- No generic Western stock photography — it destroys brand authenticity
- No perfectly staged "startup office" aesthetics — LoLe is a restaurant company
- No overlaid text directly on busy photography without a semi-transparent mist overlay
- No cold or blue-tinted color grading
- No photos of empty restaurants — convey life and activity

## Illustration Style

When custom illustration is needed (onboarding flows, empty states, feature explainers), LoLe uses a clean,
geometric flat illustration style with a warm, limited color palette derived from the brand colors.
Style Geometric flat · No outlines (filled forms) · Warm mist and green palette
